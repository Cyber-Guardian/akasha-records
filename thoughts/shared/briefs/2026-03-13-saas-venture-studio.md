# Idea Brief: SaaS Venture Studio with Founder-Friendly Economics

**Date:** 2026-03-13
**Status:** Shaped -> Parked

## Problem
Strong developers want entrepreneurial upside but don't want to do marketing/sales. AI tools have compressed build costs enough to make a portfolio of focused SaaS businesses viable — but no existing model combines founder-friendly economics (80/20 rev share) with full-time roles, proprietary dev infrastructure, and GTM support.

## Constraints
- Must attract genuinely strong developers — the model lives or dies on talent quality
- Company's 20% cut is thin; needs products at $500k+ ARR to break even per dev
- Market selection is the core competency — picking the right verticals is the hardest part
- Niche SaaS wins on depth of customer understanding, which requires focused full-time attention
- No exact precedent exists for this specific model structure

## Options Considered

### Micro SaaS Incubator (original idea)
50/50 rev share, side-project time, high volume of small products ($5k-$50k MRR).
- Gains: Low cost per bet, high volume, portfolio diversification
- Costs: Won't attract strong devs at 50/50. Side-project energy insufficient for niche depth. Volume strategy fails for building (vs acquiring) new products. 70% of micro SaaS makes under $1k/month.
- Complexity: Medium
- Evidence: Grounded against web research — structural problems on multiple axes (talent, depth, volume, moat)

### Venture Studio with Founder-Friendly Economics (evolved idea)
Full-time unstructured role, 80/20 rev share (dev keeps 80%), targeting $100k-$5M ARR per product.
- Gains: Talent attraction (better than bootstrapping solo or VC), focused depth, aligned incentives at 80%. At $500k ARR the dev makes $400k (beats FAANG) and the company breaks even. At $1M+ ARR both sides win big.
- Costs: Higher burn — salary + marketing + tooling per dev before products hit revenue. Thin margin on company's 20% requires products to reach meaningful ARR. Risk of devs leaving once products scale (though 80% + salary + harness + GTM makes staying rational).
- Complexity: High
- Closest precedents: Hexa/eFounders (30% equity, produced 3 unicorns), Betaworks (hacker-in-residence with salary), High Alpha ($125M Fund IV for enterprise SaaS studio). All use equity not rev share, but the structure rhymes.

## Chosen Approach
**Venture Studio with Founder-Friendly Economics** — the evolved version. Full-time devs, 80/20 rev share, $100k-$5M ARR targets, proprietary dev harness as speed advantage (not moat), company provides market intelligence + GTM + infrastructure.

## Key Context Discovered During Grounding
- 70% of micro SaaS businesses make under $1k/month; only 1-2% exceed $50k MRR (Rocking Web 2025 study)
- AI code has 1.7x more major issues and 2.74x more security vulnerabilities than human code (CodeRabbit analysis)
- Strong devs won't accept 50/50 — they can bootstrap solo (100%) or raise VC (give up 10-20%)
- Constellation Software proves volume works for acquiring proven businesses, not building new ones
- Tiny Capital stock down ~50% since IPO; adjusted EBITDA was negative when non-cash gains stripped out
- Calm Fund (SEAL model, closest to rev share) had sound thesis but died from management fee economics
- Hexa/eFounders: strongest studio precedent — 30% equity, 3 unicorns (Spendesk, Aircall, Front)
- Internal tools only become moats when externalized (AWS), open-sourced (Spotify Backstage), or become ecosystem standards (Shopify Polaris)
- BCG study (740 companies, 2010-2023): focused companies consistently outperform diversified
