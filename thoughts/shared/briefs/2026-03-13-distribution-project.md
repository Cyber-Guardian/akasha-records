# Idea Brief: Distribution as Strategic GTM Project

**Date:** 2026-03-13
**Status:** Shaped → Implementing (created on Linear)

## Problem
In a future where AI commoditizes product capabilities, distribution becomes a co-dependent moat alongside product. For FileScience in a channel-dominated backup market (Datto is MSP-exclusive, Veeam is 100% channel), distribution is the nearer-term constraint — great product with no distribution fails. The BizDev OS plan, channel partnership strategy, and events/community work had no organizational home on Linear.

## Constraints
- Distribution and product are co-dependent moats on different time horizons: distribution wins the 6-24 month market access race, product/data moats win the 2-5 year defensibility race
- Channel partnerships take 180 days minimum, 12-24 months to mature — this is strategic, not tactical
- Small team — initiative-level overhead risks organizational theater
- Go to Market initiative already covers commercial work for both v1 and v2 platform

## Options Considered

### New Initiative — "Distribution & BizDev"
Peer to V2 Platform, Go to Market, etc. Projects: BizDev OS, Channel Partnerships, Events & Community.
- Gains: Signals strategic priority. Separate reporting cadence. Won't get buried under v1 product work.
- Costs: Adds initiative overhead. Small team may not justify a whole initiative.
- Complexity: Medium

### New Project under Go to Market — "Distribution"
Single project with milestones for BizDev OS, Channel Partnerships, Events & Community.
- Gains: Lower overhead. GTM is the right commercial umbrella for both v1 and v2. Keeps initiative count manageable.
- Costs: Risks deprioritization alongside v1 marketing work.
- Complexity: Low

## Chosen Approach
**New Project under Go to Market** — GTM applies to both platform v1 and v2. The initiative boundary between "v1 product marketing" and "distribution" isn't worth the overhead. The project boundary within Go to Market gives distribution its own tracking without initiative inflation.
