# Idea Brief: Akasha — The Interface Substrate

**Date:** 2026-03-20 (consolidated 2026-03-21)
**Status:** Shaped → Parked (ENG-2754)
**Parent company:** Akasha
**CEO alignment:** Nick Martin reviewed pitch 2026-03-21, positive on consumer model and holdco structure

---

## 1. Founding Thesis

Akasha is the substrate between humans and their digital lives. Every app, every service, every data source — accessed through one intelligent interface that knows you completely. Akasha doesn't replace existing software. It becomes the layer through which everything else is accessed — like the browser for websites, the smartphone for services. The AI-era substrate.

> *"The company you trust to protect your data is the company you'll trust to understand your life."*

Not an app company. Not a SaaS company. **The interface company.** You don't charge for the apps. You charge for the substrate — the connectors, the context, the intelligence, the interface. Apps come and go. The substrate compounds.

---

## 2. The Problem

No platform exists that builds a unified ontology of a person's entire life — finances, health, habits, work, relationships — and makes it available to agents that coach, nudge, and automate on your behalf. Every personal AI today has a narrow window: your fitness tracker knows steps, your bank knows spending, your calendar knows meetings. Nobody connects them.

The cross-domain derivative context — sleep quality vs. spending impulses, social isolation vs. health decline, exercise frequency vs. productivity — is the intelligence no single-domain app can produce. It only exists when you connect everything.

## 3. The Product Vision

### One Core Platform, Two Products

| | FileScience (B2B) | Consumer Product (B2C) |
|---|---|---|
| Connectors | Clio, M365, Box, NetDocs | Plaid, Apple Health, Calendar, Contacts, Spotify, ... |
| Interface | Dashboard + agent (pro) | Text/call-first — the only app you need |
| Wedge | Backup (insurance) | Coaching (life intelligence) |
| Moat | Organizational memory | Personal ontology |
| Tier | Pro/premium features | Consumer, freemium ($20/mo target) |
| Agent personality | Professional, scoped | Personal, holistic, proactive |

**Same core:** Sync engine, entity graph, MCP surface, agent runtime, generative UI protocol. A connector is a connector — whether it talks to Clio or Plaid, it feeds the same graph.

### The Trajectory
Start as a coach that texts you about your budget. Evolve into the only interface you need — because it knows everything and can act on anything through write-back connectors.

- "Text me to reschedule my day."
- "Call me if something looks wrong with my health data."
- "Remind me to call my mom — it's been 3 weeks."
- "You sleep worse every time you skip the gym for 4+ days — you're at day 3."

### Long-Term: Platform-as-a-Service
The ontology engine + agent runtime becomes an open platform. FileScience and the personal product are both first-party consumers — built on the same SDK/API that third parties could also use. "Build your own life agent." The MCP surface already IS this — "use the service, make your own interface."

## 4. Corporate Structure

### Akasha Holding Company

```
Akasha Inc. (Delaware C-Corp — HoldCo)
│   Owns: core platform IP (sync engine, entity graph, MCP, agent runtime)
│   Raises: all capital at this level
│   Employs: engineering team (shared resource)
│
├── CGCG LLC — consulting/services (existing revenue)
├── FileScience LLC — B2B backup + org intelligence (existing revenue)
├── [Consumer Product] LLC — B2C life ontology (future)
└── [Future products] — all built on the same core
```

**Why holdco:** Investors get diversified exposure. Core platform IP is protected upstream. Each subsidiary has isolated liability. QSBS multiplication ($10M exclusion per qualifying sub per shareholder). Consolidated tax return nets losses against profits across subs.

**IP ownership:** Core platform IP sits at HoldCo, licensed to subsidiaries via formal arm's-length agreements (IRS Section 482). R&D services model: subsidiaries assign newly developed IP to parent.

**VC perception:** Frame as "platform company with unified customer thesis and multiple product expressions" — not "holding company." Target VCs who've backed multi-product companies, or non-VC capital (family offices, angels, RBF).

### Key Decision from Nick (2026-03-21)
- "If we raise we shouldn't raise on the smaller platforms. Should raise in the hold co."
- "The consumer model is interesting" — agrees on omni-product, same platform for both markets.
- Consumer support concern resolved: "it's an AI agent platform, we just make support agents."

## 5. Financing Strategy

### Nick's Question: Debt vs Equity
Nick is "still not convinced debt vs equity." The answer: **both, matched to risk profile.**

### The Hybrid Play (Recommended)

| Entity | Business | Instrument | Rationale |
|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| B2B OpCo (FileScience) | Backup SaaS | MRR credit facility (5-8x MRR) | Predictable cash flow; zero dilution |
| HoldCo (Akasha) | Platform + subs | Modest equity raise (SAFE) | Funds consumer product; valued against BOTH businesses |
| Consumer OpCo | Personal product | Burns holdco capital | Pre-revenue; equity is the correct instrument |

**The key frame:** "We don't dilute the operating business at all. Zero equity given away on FileScience. The equity happens at the holdco level, where the valuation reflects both B2B revenue AND consumer upside."

### Non-Dilutive Options (B2B Side)

| Provider | Min ARR | Max | Cost | Dilution |
|----------|---------|-----|------|----------|
| Lighter Capital | $200K | $4M | 1.3-1.5x cap (18-35% APR) | None |
| Capchase | $1M | $10M (70% ARR) | Flat fee, lower than venture debt | None |
| MRR Credit Facility | $2.5M | $20M | 13-16% interest | None |
| Revenue Participation Notes | Any | Negotiable | % of revenue until cap | None |

### Equity (HoldCo Level)
- SAFE (post-money): 90% of pre-seed deals. No maturity, no interest.
- Current caps: $3-10M pre-seed, $6-15M seed.
- The B2B revenue anchors a higher valuation, minimizing dilution.

### Nick's "Bonds" Idea — Real Instruments
- **Revenue Participation Notes (RPNs):** Investor gets % of revenue until 1.5x cap. Bond-like. No equity, no board seats.
- **Wefunder Revenue Share:** Crowdfunded version. Community-building + zero dilution.
- **Mezzanine financing:** 15-25% interest + equity conversion option if default. For the holdco level.

### Guardrails
- No cross-default clauses between B2B debt and holdco
- No MAC clause without defined thresholds
- No full-ratchet anti-dilution on holdco equity
- No super pro-rata rights for consumer investors
- Keep debt and equity stacks at separate entity levels

### Models to Study
- **Tiny Capital (Andrew Wilkinson):** MetaLab was the cash engine; profits reinvested. Zero VC. Went public 2023 via RTO. Radical decentralization.
- **Constellation Software (Mark Leonard):** Started with $25M from pension fund. Never diluted post-IPO. Buys profitable VMS companies at 1.25x revenue.
- Both bootstrapped — neither took traditional VC. The holdco model becomes available once one product generates enough free cash flow to fund the others.

## 7. The Moat

### Four Compounding Advantages
1. **Cold start solved** — trust earned through backup, connections already established. The company you trust to undo anything becomes the company you trust to know everything.
2. **Temporal irreplaceability** — clone our features, but not 2 years of someone's life patterns. Every day we run, the moat deepens.
3. **Derivative context** — cross-domain intelligence that exists only on our platform. No single app generates it. Only the substrate can.
4. **Trust ladder** — backup trust → read access → coaching trust → action trust. Each step earned, not assumed.

### The Derivative Context Moat (Detailed)
Raw connectors are table stakes. The value is in what you **generate across domains**:
- **Cross-domain correlations:** Sleep quality vs. spending impulses. Exercise frequency vs. productivity. Social isolation vs. health decline.
- **Temporal patterns:** Not just "what is" but "what's changing." Trend lines across every life domain.
- **Life events detected, not declared:** The platform notices you're job hunting (LinkedIn spike + courses + resume edits) before you tell it.
- **Predictive nudges:** "Based on your patterns, you're about to overspend this month."

## 8. Personal Data Landscape

### Nine Life Domains

| Domain | Connect (APIs) | Generate (Derivative Context) |
|--------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| **Financial** | Plaid, Stripe/Square, crypto wallets, Venmo, credit bureaus | Spending patterns, budget projections, net worth trajectory, subscription inventory |
| **Health** | Apple Health, Oura/Whoop, FHIR/Epic MyChart, MyFitnessPal | Sleep-exercise correlation, health trend alerts, cross-domain insights |
| **Time** | Google Calendar, Toggl, Screen Time, Todoist | Deep work ratio, meeting load, task completion velocity |
| **Relationships** | Contacts, call/text metadata, social media, email patterns | Relationship health scores, social circle mapping, isolation alerts |
| **Career** | LinkedIn, GitHub, Slack, payroll, learning platforms | Skills inventory, career trajectory, income progression |
| **Home** | HomeKit, Google Home, utilities, weather, location | Energy patterns, maintenance predictions, habit analysis |
| **Education** | Kindle/Readwise, podcasts, YouTube, courses, Anki | Learning velocity, knowledge graph, retention estimates |
| **Legal** | Google Drive, insurance, government services, TurboTax | Document expiry tracking, compliance calendar |
| **Entertainment** | Spotify, Netflix, gaming, travel, restaurants | Taste profiles, entertainment budget, behavioral patterns |

### API Feasibility (from research)

**Buildable today via server-side REST APIs (no native app required):**
- Financial: Plaid (OAuth2, REST, sandbox available, ~$1.50/user/mo)
- Calendar: Google Calendar API, Microsoft Graph API (personal accounts)
- Productivity: Todoist API (REST + Sync)
- Email: Gmail API (metadata mode)
- Wearables: Oura Ring API, WHOOP API (both true REST, OAuth2)
- Smart home: Google Nest SDM API ($5 one-time)
- Health records: Epic FHIR (SMART on FHIR, free, 195M patients via MyChart)

**Requires native iOS/Android app:**
- Apple HealthKit (on-device only — no server API)
- Android Health Connect (on-device only — Google Fit REST API shut down June 2025)
- Apple HomeKit (on-device only)
- Shortcut: Terra API ($399/mo) bridges most wearables including HealthKit/Health Connect

**Not available on iOS:**
- Call log metadata, SMS metadata (Apple blocks entirely; Android allows with permissions)

### Data Capture Tiers

| Tier | Scope | Trust bar |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 1 — Digital exhaust | Structured APIs you already use | Medium — permissioned |
| 2 — Active logging | Opt-in tracking (mood, food, voice memos) | Lower — user chooses |
| 3 — Ambient observation | Passive sensing (location, screen time) | Very high — surveillant feel |

Start Tier 1. Let derivative context prove value. Users opt into Tier 2/3 as trust builds.

### Regulatory Landscape
- **CFPB Section 1033** (US): compliance deadline June 30, 2026 — US banks will open standardized data APIs
- **EU DMA Article 6(9):** Gatekeepers (Apple, Google, Meta) must provide continuous, real-time data portability APIs
- **GDPR/CCPA:** Personal data aggregation is regulated but not prohibited — consent and purpose limitation are key
- **HIPAA-adjacent:** Health data from consumer wearables is NOT HIPAA-protected; clinical data from FHIR/Epic is

## 10. Consumer Product Naming

### Methodology
Applied professional naming playbooks (Lexicon, Igor, Wolff Olins) + sound symbolism research + 65+ candidate generation across 5 linguistic methods.

### The Science
- **Nasals (m, n):** warmth, nurturing, positive valence. The "ma" sound = comfort, maternal protection.
- **Liquids (l, r):** fluid, elegant, smooth. Luxury brands cluster here.
- **Open vowels (a, o):** approachable, substantial. Back vowels = warm.
- **Open syllables (end in vowel):** friendly, warm. Spotify, Canva, Oreo.
- **CVCV pattern:** 37% higher recall. Universal across languages. First words children say.
- **2 syllables, 3-5 letters:** optimal for text messaging, voice recognition, App Store.
- **No "AI" in name:** 23% find it uninspiring. It dates.
- **No bolt-on prefixes (get, hi):** 96% credibility penalty.
- **Gender-neutral trending:** trust ratings don't differ between gendered and gender-neutral AI voices, but gender-neutral produces fewer impolite interactions.

### Final Five Candidates

| Name | Letters | Origin | Why It Works |
|------|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Alma** | 4 | "soul" (Spanish), "nourishing" (Latin) | Cross-culturally rich. "Hey Alma" is warm. Means "soul" to 500M+ native speakers. |
| **Ora** | 3 | "light" (Hebrew), "alive" (Maori), "speak" (Latin) | Triple meaning convergence. Shortest candidate. Oracle-adjacent. |
| **Lumi** | 4 | "luminous" (Latin), "snow" (Finnish) | Bright, friendly, gender-neutral. Clean phonetics. |
| **Miru** | 4 | "to see" (Japanese) | The AI that truly sees you. Mirror/mind blend. |
| **Valo** | 4 | "light" (Finnish) | Fresh, warm, minimal conflicts. Neutral cultural load. |

### Practical Constraints
- .com: 95% of SaaS uses .com. .ai acceptable for AI products.
- App Store: 30-character title limit. Use subtitle for "AI" keyword.
- USPTO: 11.5M+ records. 3-letter marks heavily saturated. Need Classes 9 + 42.
- Quick screen: namecheckly.com for domain + social handles.

## 11. Text/Call-First Interface

The agent becomes the only app. Users text and call it — not a dashboard, not an app. Combined with the generative UI protocol from the Akasha Capability Platform, the agent renders interactive UI inline in messaging (Apple Messages for Business, WhatsApp Flows) while maintaining conversational interaction for everything else.

**Channel architecture:**
- iMessage (Apple Messages for Business): Forms (iOS 18.4+), Apple Pay, native OAuth, rich interactive elements
- WhatsApp Flows: 3B users, cross-platform, comparable interactive forms
- Voice calls: for complex or emotional coaching moments

**The "only app" trajectory:**
1. Text-based coaching (budget alerts, health trends, reminders)
2. Stream-of-consciousness idea dump — text the agent like you'd text a friend or post in Slack. Unstructured thoughts, half-formed ideas, random observations. The agent captures, organizes, and connects everything to your ontology. "I keep thinking about switching jobs" becomes a tracked life thread with correlated data (LinkedIn activity, salary trends, calendar patterns). Your inner monologue becomes structured context over time — the agent notices themes before you do.
3. Interactive UI in chat (generative components for scheduling, settings, approvals)
4. Voice calls for nuanced conversations
5. Write-back actions through connectors ("reschedule my day", "pay this bill", "reply to that email")

## 12. Options Considered

### Shared Core, Two Brands (Chosen — Near-Term)
One codebase, one platform, two products with separate branding and connector sets.
- Gains: Maximum code reuse, fastest to ship, one team builds both
- Costs: B2B and B2C have different go-to-market and trust models
- Complexity: Medium

### Platform-as-a-Service (Chosen — Long-Term)
The ontology engine + agent runtime is an open platform. FileScience and Personal are both first-party consumers.
- Gains: Network effects, developer ecosystem, venture narrative ("the Shopify of personal AI")
- Costs: Platform AND two products simultaneously
- Complexity: High

### Personal-First, B2B Follows (Rejected)
Launch consumer product first.
- Gains: Virality, strongest brand story
- Costs: Consumer acquisition is expensive, personal data trust is hardest to earn
- Why rejected: FileScience B2B must fund the core. Consumer can't come first.
