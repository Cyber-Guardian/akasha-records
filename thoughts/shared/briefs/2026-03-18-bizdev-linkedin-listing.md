# Idea Brief: Business Development LinkedIn Listing

**Date:** 2026-03-18
**Status:** Shaped → Implementing

## Problem
Filescience needs a first business hire to co-develop and execute GTM strategy alongside the CEO. The backup market is structurally channel-driven (Datto MSP-exclusive, Veeam 100% channel) and channel partnerships take 180+ days to mature — starting now is critical. Current gap: CEO sets strategy but has limited execution time; sales director closes but doesn't self-direct strategy or outbound. No one owns partnerships, product positioning, events, or the weekly BizDev operating rhythm.

## Constraints
- Budget: $50-75k (25th percentile for BizDev, below market for the actual scope)
- Must attract junior talent (1-3 years out) willing to bet on equity upside
- In-office, NYC
- LinkedIn title must be "Business Development" (not "BizDev") for algorithm searchability
- Tone: startup-y, direct, no corporate fluff — matches existing engineering listing
- Equity mentioned but range kept vague

## Options Considered

### "Right Hand" Framing
Lead with CEO relationship and access. Emphasizes career opportunity over specific tasks.
- Gains: Attracts ambitious generalists, differentiates from generic listings
- Costs: Might attract title-seekers, reads vague on actual work
- Complexity: Low

### "Channel Builder" Framing
Lead with market reality — backup is 100% channel, build our partner ecosystem.
- Gains: Self-selects for relevant experience, clear deliverables
- Costs: Undersells strategic scope, reads more senior than budget supports
- Complexity: Low

### "Startup Generalist" Framing
Lead with breadth — first business hire, touch everything, whatever matters most that week.
- Gains: Attracts exact profile (scrappy, adaptable), honest about catch-all nature, works at junior comp level
- Costs: Could attract unfocused candidates, less LinkedIn-searchable
- Complexity: Low

## Chosen Approach
**Hybrid — Startup Generalist energy + Channel Builder specifics.** LinkedIn title "Business Development" for searchability. Listing body uses generalist voice with channel-building anchored in "What You'll Do." CEO direct-report in "Why Join Us" not the lead. Sell the opportunity, not the comp.
